Diploma in Sustainable Development

This course gives students an understanding of the key challenges and pathways to sustainable development - that is, economic development that is also socially inclusive and environmentally sustainable.


Syllabus:

Lecture 1: What is Sustainable Development?
  • Chapter 1: Introduction to Sustainable Development
  • Chapter 2: Economic Growth and Progress
  • Chapter 3: Continuing Poverty
  • Chapter 4: Environmental Threats
  • Chapter 5: Business As Usual Versus Sustainable Development

Lecture 2: Economic Development - How We Measure It, How It Varies Around the World
  • Chapter 1: Incomes Around the World
  • Chapter 2: Urban/Rural Inequality
  • Chapter 3: Income Inequality Within Countries
  • Chapter 4: Measuring Wellbeing
  • Chapter 5: Convergence or Divergence?

Lecture 3: A Short History of Economic Development
  • Chapter 1: Economic Development is New, Starting Around 1750
  • Chapter 2: The Industrial Revolution Starts in England
  • Chapter 3: The Great Waves of Technological Change
  • Chapter 4: The Diffusion of Economic Growth
  • Chapter 5: Economic Development Since World War II: The Making of Globalization

Lecture 4: Why Did Some Countries Advance While Others Remained in Poverty
  • Chapter 1: The Idea of Clinical Economics
  • Chapter 2: The Role of Physical Geography: Transport, Energy, Disease, Crops
  • Chapter 3: The Role of Culture: Demography, Education, Gender
  • Chapter 4: The Role of Politics
  • Chapter 5: Which Countries Are Still Stuck in Poverty?

Lecture 5: The MDGs and the End of Extreme Poverty
  • Chapter 1: The Reasons to Believe that Extreme Poverty Can Be Ended
  • Chapter 2: A Strategy to End Extreme Poverty in Africa
  • Chapter 3: South Asia: The Continuing Challenge of the Food Supply
  • Chapter 4: A Closer Look at Official Development Assistance
  • Chapter 5: Designing Practical Interventions: The Case of Millennium Villages

Lecture 6: Growth within Planetary Boundaries
  • Chapter 1: The Planetary Boundaries
  • Chapter 2: Growth Dynamics
  • Chapter 3: Growth and Planetary Boundaries: The Case of Energy
  • Chapter 4: Growth and Planetary Boundaries: The Case of Food
  • Chapter 5: Growth and Planetary Boundaries: The Case of Population

Lecture 7: Human Rights and Gender Equality
  • Chapter 1: The Ethics of Wealth, Poverty, and Inequality
  • Chapter 2: Major UN Covenants and Declarations
  • Chapter 3: Divided Societies
  • Chapter 4: Forces of Widening Inequalities
  • Chapter 5: Gender Inequity

Lecture 8: Education
  • Chapter 1: Life-Cycle Approach to Human Development
  • Chapter 2: Early Childhood Development
  • Chapter 3: The Rising Returns to Education and the Supply Response
  • Chapter 4: Social Mobility
  • Chapter 5: The Role of Higher Education in Sustainable Development

Lecture 9: Universal Health Coverage
  • Chapter 1: The Human Right to Health
  • Chapter 2: Poverty and Disease
  • Chapter 3: Designing and Financing a Primary Health System in Low-Income Settings
  • Chapter 4: Ten Recommended Steps to Health for All in the Poorest Countries
  • Chapter 5: The Challenges of Health Coverage in High-Income Countries

Lecture 10: Sustainable Food Supply and the End of Hunger
  • Chapter 1: Malnutrition
  • Chapter 2: Farm Systems, Ecology, and Food Security
  • Chapter 3: How Environmental Change Threatens the Food System
  • Chapter 4: How the Food System Threatens the Environment
  • Chapter 5: Towards a Sustainable Global Food Supply

Lecture 11: Sustainable Cities
  • Chapter 1: The Patterns of Urbanization Around the World
  • Chapter 2: What Makes a City Sustainable?
  • Chapter 3: Smart Infrastructure
  • Chapter 4: Urban Resilience
  • Chapter 5: Planning for Sustainable Development

Lecture 12: Curbing Climate Change
  • Chapter 1: The Basic Science of Climate Change
  • Chapter 2: Consequences
  • Chapter 3: Mitigation
  • Chapter 4: Adaptation
  • Chapter 5: Mitigation Policies

Lecture 3 & 4 Month- Two

Diploma in Statistical Analysis-SPSS for Under-grade Students

Course description:

SPSS is a package that many beginners and power users like because SPSS offers a good combination of ease to learn and yet very powerful to work. SPSS statistical data software is a complete, integrated statistical software package that provides for data analysis, data management, and graphics etc. SPSS is not solid in modules, meaning that we can get everything we need in one package. The course is aimed at researchers and other health care professionals who would like to strengthen their capacity using this statistical data analysis software.

This 12-day (36 hours) training will enhance the knowledge and skills of the participants to analyze data in a fast, accurate and easier manner by using SPSS software and will know how to interpret and present the results. This will also enable participants to gain experience of the theory and practical application of the statistical analytical techniques.


Why SPSS:
  • SPSS is fast, accurate, and easy to use
  • It has broad suite of statistical features
  • It provides complete data-management facilities
  • As SPSS gives publication- quality graphics
  • It is both responsive and extensible and matrix programming
  • SPSS is easy to get complete documentation and other publications
  • SPSS is affordable and widely used software.

Course contents:
  • Brief knowledge about statistics and familiarizing with SPSS
  • Data entry, importing external data files
  • Data Management
  • Descriptive statistics
  • Graphical representations
  • Test of hypothesis
  • Analysis of Variance
  • Correlation
  • Regression (Linear, Logistic)
  • Survival Analysis
  • Principal components & Factor Analysis
  • Time Series Analysis

Diploma in Public Health

Background:

University of Sustainable Development (USD) has always been striving for excellence in ensuring quality and required domains of knowledge. USD truly holds the belief that in the era of high technological inclusion in all the sectors, particularly education, distance is no longer a barrier of receiving a full-fledged quality education. With an aim to contribute to building adroit human resources in the health sector and other associated sectors along with meeting the thirst for knowledge of the enthusiastic learners, the dynamic leadership of Frontier University has undertaken an exemplary step of offering the incredible opportunity of distance learning using the limitless potentials of technological advancement.

This instrumental course has been designed and developed to deliver an understanding of the principles of public health and how it relates to our social life. The course encompasses a broad variety of topic areas including the ground understanding regarding the concept and the history of public health, social determinants of health, multi-disciplinary approach of public health, and health education & promotion related affairs.


Mode of Class Lecture:

Delivery of the class lectures held online involves a variety of teaching methods, including group discussions, group presentations, individual lectures etc.


Duration: Four Months

Structure:
  • First Month: Lecture-01
  • Second Month: Lecture-02
  • Third Month: Lecture-03
  • Fourth Month: Lecture-04

Class Lecture Delivery and Structure

Lectures consist of computer-based presentations with audio and video, and incorporate self-assessment and interactive elements where appropriate. Lecture material is supplemented by a program of directed study, including review papers and original research publications. Students are provided with a comprehensive portfolio of reading material in electronic format.

Each course includes regular assignments and a formal examination to be completed by students. In addition to the coursework contained in these modules, students are offered the opportunity to participate in an annual residential meeting. Some of the eminent resource persons will be involved in the development and delivery of material, and will be made accessible via online chats and the residential workshop. Upon successful completion of all compulsory elements of the course, including coursework, assignments and examinations, students are eligible to receive validated certificate from USD.


Course Contents:
Lecture 1: Landscape of Public Health
  1. The historical Background of Public Health.
  2. Eminent Personalities and their unparalleled contribution to the field of Public health
  3. Ground understanding regarding some essential issues of Public Health.
  4. Terminologies with visual presentations

Lecture 2: Social Determinants of Health
  1. Social factors associated with Health.
  2. Brief description of all the factors.
  3. Explanation of how our health is shaped by external catalysts.
  4. Community Role in preventing pandemic, COVID-19.

Lecture 3: Multi-disciplinary Approach of Public Health
  1. Major disciplines of Public Health
  2. Paradigm shift of the Public Health domain
  3. Holistic Approach of Health
  4. One Health & Sustainable Development Goals

Lecture 4: Health Education & Promotion
  1. Concept and role of Health Education
  2. Behaviour Change Communication (BCC)
  3. Social Behaviour Change Communication (SBCC)
  4. WASH (Water, Sanitation and Hygiene)

Lecture 5: Burden of Disease and Public Health Challenges in Somalia
  1. Concept of Global Burden of Disease (GBD)
  2. BoD (Burden of Disease) in Africa and Somalia
  3. Female Genital Mutilation (FGM) in Africa and Somalia
  4. Malnutrition in African and Somalia
  5. Emerging Health Conditions in Africa and Somalia

Award of Attendance:

After the effective participation in the course, the attendees will receive a certificate from University of Sustainable Development (USD)
